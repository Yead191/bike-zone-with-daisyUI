Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

Replace your helmet if it’s over 5 years old, shows signs of damage (cracks, dents, worn straps), no longer fits snugly, or has been involved in a crash. Additionally, consider upgrading if newer models with advanced safety features are available.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

Before riding a stored bike, check tire pressure, brakes, chain condition, and inspect the frame for damage. Ensure gears shift smoothly and lubricate the chain if needed.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

When riding your bike, follow these rules: - Obey all traffic signals and signs. - Ride in the same direction as traffic. - Use hand signals for turns and stops. - Wear a helmet and bright or reflective clothing. - Stay in bike lanes when available. - Be aware of your surroundings and avoid distractions.
